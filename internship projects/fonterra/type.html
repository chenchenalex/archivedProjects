Pot Set Yoghurt

Milk is prepared and treated as for stirred yoghurt manufacture until the point of fermentation.  The inoculated milk is well mixed then used to fill retail containers and fermented in the individual pottles. Once the fermentation is complete the yoghurt pottles are cooled and ready for distribution. (Protein range: 3-4 %)

Stirred or smoothed yoghurt

Stirred or smoothed yoghurt is yoghurt that is fermented in a bulk tank prior to being stirred or smoothed using specific equipment such as a back pressure valve or high shear device.  Lower solids products may be sufficiently smoothed by agitation (i.e. stirring) in the tank and/or pumping i.e. transfer to the filling lines.  The smoothing/stirring action gives the yoghurt a homogeneous, flowable consistency.  After stirring/smoothing the yoghurt may be combined with fruit (optional) then filled into the final retail pots. (Protein range: 3-5 %)

Pot Set Yoghurt

Concentrated or higher protein spoonable cultured products such as Greek (style) yoghurt, Petit Suisse, Fromage Frais and Quark were manufactured traditionally by using a whey separation (‘strained’) process that concentrates the solids after fermentation.  Today, manufacturers are able to make these types of products using a direct set quick process with the aid of Fonterra MPC ingredients. Petit Suisse, Fromage Frais and Quark are similar types of products and can be described as soft, cultured, un-ripened fresh cheese made using a mesophillic culture, and sometimes rennet.  The texture is thick, smooth and creamy and the product is typically eaten as a dessert or snack.  These products come with varying fat and protein contents and are sold plain, or sweetened and fruited, or as savoury versions with salt or herbs. The direct set quick process increases the milk solids at the start of the process (i.e. during recombining), rather than concentrating after fermentation.  This means that existing yoghurt manufacturing equipment can generally be used, eliminating the need for major capital investment and avoiding the acid whey waste stream that is generated during the traditional strained process.  Cream and sugar can be added to the milk before culturing as the target composition will be maintained by eliminating the separation (dewheying) process step. (Protein range: 5-10 %)

Pot Set Yoghurt

Drinking yoghurts are normally a blend of yoghurt, sugar and fruit juice or fruit flavour/pulp.  The concentration of milk solids and protein may be various. The fermented yoghurt curd needs to be processed through high shear to make the yogurt product pourable and drinkable. The protein may need to be stabilised by stabilizers to prevent sedimentation. (Protein range: 1-8 %)
